# Sprint Retrospective

A structured checklist for running a safe, blameless sprint retrospective — designed to continuously improve team effectiveness, remove blockers, reinforce positive behaviours, and drive actionable improvements into future sprints.

---

## Objective

- Establish a consistent, repeatable retrospective format
- Ensure psychological safety and open participation
- Capture actionable improvements — not just observations
- Track improvements across sprints

Estimated duration: 60 minutes (including preparation and follow-up).

---

## When to Use

- At the end of every sprint or iteration
- Whenever the team needs a structured space to reflect and reset
- As part of a broader Sprint Ceremonies Kit

---

## Structure Overview

1. Before the Retro — scheduling, invites, and reviewing previous actions
2. Set the Stage — establish a safe, blameless environment
3. Gather Data — what went well, what didn't, what surprised us
4. Generate Insights — patterns, root causes, and discussion
5. Decide Actions — 1–3 concrete, owned improvements
6. Close — summarise, gather feedback, reinforce accountability
7. After the Retro — capture, prioritise, and share

---

## Suggested Ritual

- Review previous retro actions before the meeting begins
- Rotate the facilitator each sprint to share ownership
- Timebox each section to keep the retro focused
- Limit actions to 1–3 per sprint to maintain focus
- Do not skip the "After the Retro" section — unlogged actions are lost actions

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- Turning the retro into a complaint session
- Leaving without actionable outcomes
- Agreeing on too many actions (lack of focus)
- Actions with no clear owner
- Not reviewing previous actions at the start
- Blame-focused discussions

---

## Import Instructions

1. Download `template.csv`
2. Create a new project in Todoist
3. Import from CSV
4. Rename the project to: `Sprint Retrospective – Sprint [N]`
5. Repeat for each sprint (typically every 1–2 weeks depending on your sprint length)
